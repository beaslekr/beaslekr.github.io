My classmates made me overall more cognizant of the upsides to social media and bots–I’ve come at the topic typically from a very anti-technology standpoint because of its allowance of global power concentration within corrupt institutions, ignoring its other contexts and aspects. For instance, in the discussion of social bots as pertaining to ethics/utilitarianism, people brought up platforms with minimal malicious bots and many helpful bots, like Discord and Slack. Another example is when people mentioned how social media was also doing its intended job of connecting people and communities.


The pre-class readings shaped my thinking by giving me more understanding of the inner workings and providers of bot networks (Socialbot Network Prep) and how difficult it is to obtain those same connections and materials as an activist rather than as a large corporation (Botivist Prep).


One lingering question I have is about how the vast uncertainty of how the future will play out with regards to new technologies is reflected in different moral frameworks–for instance, is there moral weight against introducing unpredictability into a system? Or is that a moral positive?
